Recommended items to bring to Mexico:

  • Travel Documentation and photocopies (Required)
  • Passport
  • Visa documentation
  • Support documentation provided by Fundar.
  • Travel insurance documentation
  • Health documents (proof of vaccinations, medical prescriptions, etc.)
  • Airline ticket
  • Telephone numbers and address of your country’s embassy and consulate in Mexico, or in the USA.

Clothing

  • Formal and casual wear appropriate for cool and warm weather (between 15 and 30°C)
    • Sweater and/or Warm jacket for chilly and rainy nights
    • Raincoat
    • Light clothing (light and short-sleeves shirts) as it can get very warm
  • Passport
  • Appropriate and comfortable clothing for field visits, sightseeing and air conditioned bus travel (light casual pants, light casual shirt with sleeves; closed-toe boots or tennis)
  • We will be visiting public agencies and policy makers, and there might be a press conference on November 4th, so we recommend that you wear formal clothes on that day. The rest of the days you can wear casual clothes.

Miscellaneous Items

  • International adapter plug (In Mexico, plugs are Type A & B)
  • Pouch to store money underneath clothing
  • Extra money for amenities not covered by ELLA study tour as listed in the \Study Tour Logistical Information\ section
  • Avoid as possible bringing items of significant value
  • Camera
  • Any medication you might need and your prescriptions
  • Sunscreen
  • Notebook or personal computer to take notes during the visit
  • We will be having a Cultural Welcome Diner on the second day of the Study Tour. This is why we invite you to bring a typical object, a piece of clothing or anything that is representative of your country and that you wish to share with us.

Some advice while travelling in Mexico:

  • Be sure to use taxi services at night time. Likewise, always take a safe taxi (taxis de sitio in Spanish). These taxis need to be called, are from trustworthy companies, and are 100% safe. Participants can ask for a safe taxi in the hotel’s front desk. Fundar staff can also help you with calling a safe taxi. Never take a taxi from the street; these are quite dangerous, so please make sure to call one of these “taxis de sitio”.
  • At night, avoid going out alone. It is preferable to go in groups of at least two people. Never talk to strangers, even if they look nice and charming.
  • Please, be advised not to take out valuable belongings (cameras, Iphones, Ipods, etc.) in the street or in crowded places.
  • Always pay with small notes, especially in small shops. Do not pay with notes of $500 pesos, unless you are in bigger stores, as it will be difficult that people have change.
  • When buying local art craft or souvenirs in a market, bargain! Since you are a tourist, vendors will try to give you higher prices. Also, bargaining is a common practice in Mexico.
  • Be sure to always carry Embassy/Consulate information, as well as Hotel (see the “Study Tour Accommodation Information” section), Fundar and ELLA staff information (see the “Emergecy Contacts” section)
